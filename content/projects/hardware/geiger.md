## About

How to make your own Geiger counter:

- Purchase a kit from [DIYGeigerCounter](https://sites.google.com/site/diygeigercounter/home)
- Purchase a geiger counter tube from [Electronic Goldmine](http://www.goldmine-elec-products.com/)
- Assemble the electronics
- Put it in a case, such as mine on [Thingiverse](https://www.thingiverse.com/thing:191113) or your own
- Test it with a uranium-glazed pottery piece from [Ebay](https://www.ebay.com/)

![Side view of the geiger-counter, without the lid on](/projects/hardware-projects/geiger-counter/geiger_side.jpg "Side view of the geiger-counter, without the lid on")
![The Geiger counter, in operation](/projects/hardware-projects/geiger-counter/geiger_top.jpg "The Geiger counter, in operation")

This case stores 6-AA's, the main electronics, the geiger-counter tube (as a snap-on component), and various interactive components, such as the power switch (left side) and brightness control (right side).

![Geiger counter electronics, without the case](/projects/hardware-projects/geiger-counter/electronics.jpg "Geiger counter electronics, without the case")

## Case Design

This was one of the first useful projects I designed and printed with my 3D printer. As such, the design was partly squished because I had to fit the entire case design within the build plate of my 3D printer.

This design is also quite dated, but is still functional.

![Geiger Counter lid (render)](/projects/hardware-projects/geiger-counter/render_lid.png "Geiger Counter lid (render)")
![Geiger Counter tube holder (wireframe)](/projects/hardware-projects/geiger-counter/wire_tube.png "Geiger Counter tube holder (wireframe)")
![Geiger Counter tube holder (render)](/projects/hardware-projects/geiger-counter/render_tube.png "Geiger Counter tube holder (render)")

These models were designed using [BRL-CAD](https://brlcad.org/), which gave me a scripting interface to create the models along with a graphical output view. With this design:

- The geiger counter base separates the battery storage from the electronics compartment
- The geiger counter lid u-pipe holds the geiger counter tube as a snap-in item
- The geiger counter tube holder has several holes, permitting charged particles to pass through

There are certainly several enhancements I'd make if I were recreating this design (or printing it in bulk), but overall the entire design worked as expected.
